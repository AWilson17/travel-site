Berlin

Entering Don's territory of Germany where he spent extended periods of time for work and speaks German, they hit Berlin first. They'll take a 4-hour tour of Berlin to get an overview of the city. Drive past the Bradenberg Gate, built as a triumphal arch for King Frederick Wilhelm II. The arch leads to the elegant Unter Den Linden Boulevard, which has seen much revitalization in recent years. Nearby, you will see Potsdam Square and the Museum Island. Other highlights in the former East Berlin include the Tiergarten Park, Bellevue Palace, the German Parliament building, and Checkpoint Charlie, the famous crossing point between East and West Berlin during the Cold War, which now houses a museum.

Bamburg

Enjoy your evening in Bamberg. One of the best undiscovered cities in this part of Germany, Bamberg was once the seat of the most powerful ruling family in the country. Having survived the ravages of war, Bamberg boasts a rich artistic heritage with a range of architectural styles represented. Perhaps begin exploring the lower city in the Maxplatz where you will find Neues Rathhaus and a daily market. Walking past the church of St. Martin, you can cross the Regnitz River and come to the imposing Domplatz. Behold one of the finest squares in Germany with its 11th-century cathedral, sculptures and museums. With the country’s largest preserved Old Town, Bamberg has more than 2000 monuments and historical hallmarks, including the ancient Gardeners’ Quarter and the old town hall. The town also has several museums and catacombs to explore.

Garmisch

Situated at the foot of the Wetterstein mountain range is Garmisch-Partenkirchen, Germany's leading ski resort. The town of Garmisch-Partenkirchen is actually composed of two towns, Garmisch and Partenkirchen, which grew up on either side of the railway lines. Garmisch has a more cosmopolitan, modern atmosphere, while Partenkirchen retains more of its simple alpine village charm.

Set in the heart of Partenkirchen and surrounded by two gardens, the moderate five-star Partenkirchner Hof was built in 1911. Designed in classic chalet style, the hotel consists of three interconnected buildings with bright flower boxes adorning the carved wood facade. The hotel features is a gourmet restaurant and informal cellar bar, serving Bavarian specialties. There are 61 guest rooms, which are decorated in a range of styles from modern to traditional. All rooms are large and feature satellite television, complimentary WiFi, in-room safe, minibar, and bathroom with hairdryer and robes. Guests may enjoy the indoor swimming pool, sauna, room service and bicycle rental.

These two days are open for exploring the Bavarian Alps. There are two ‘must see’ options that we suggest for these two days: Zugspitze and Neuschwanstein.

Rothenburg

Enjoy a free day in Rothenburg. One of the most charming cities in Germany, Rothenburg-ob-der- Tauber dates mainly to the 17th century. Surrounding the town is more than a mile of walls, with stairs every 200 – 300 yards, which you can climb for the view. The Rathaus or Town Hall is probably the best place to begin your explorations, as it is the heart of the town (Rathausplatz). Nearby is the Markplatz, which features the Herterlich Fountain, while the town’s main churches are nearby. There are several museums, including the Imperial City Museum (housed in a former convent), the Medieval Criminal Museum (displaying instruments of torture.)

Ruedesheim

One of the Rhine Valley’s most popular towns, Ruedesheim is a quaint spot that is often filled with tourists. With half-timbered and gabled medieval houses, wine cellars, rustic restaurants, and a riverside promenade, the Ruedesheim summarily represents towns of the Reisling wine region. Highlights include the Weingass (Wine Alley) and Drosselgasse (Thrush Lane), which run through the heart of the Old Town. These narrow cobblestoned streets are lined with wine taverns and classic German restaurants. You can also visit Bromserburg Castle, which was built over 1,000 years ago and now houses a small wine museum. For excellent views of the river and countryside, you can take a chairlift up to the Niederwald Memorial – a massive statue set 1,000 feet above the town to celebrate the unification of the German Empire in 1871.

Rhine River Valley

Enjoy a day out on the magnificent Rhine River with your one-day cruise pass. There are usually 2-3 departures in the morning and 2 in the afternoon so you go at your own pace, exploring enchanting little towns and sites along the way. Choose to leave early and take the cruise in both directions or depart later, returning to your originating town by taxi or train. The choice is yours!

On the cruise, simply relax as you pass scenic countryside, many old fortresses and charming villages. A taped commentary offers explanations about the famous sights, such as the Loreley Rock, the fortresses of Rheinfels, Stolzenfels and Ehrenbreitstein and many more. Between Koblenz and Ruedesheim, stops include Boppard, St. Goarshausen, St. Goar, Oberwesel, and Bacharach amongst others; this is the most scenic portion of the river.
